Intro

Empowering and nurturing new talent

Watersprite is one of a kind. Since 2008, Watersprite has celebrated the most groundbreaking student films from over 100 countries worldwide. Not only is the festival almost entirely run by students for students, but the Festival firmly believes promoting access and alleviating barriers to entry into the film industry for student filmmakers from all walks of life. To this end, Watersprite provides international filmmakers with travel bursaries to Cambridge, in addition to offering free access to screenings, workshops, mentorship schemes and Q&As attended by leading industry experts.

Mentorship programmes

The WOMEN AND NON-BINARY MENTORSHIP

For filmmakers who identify as women, non-binary, genderqueer, and/or gender nonconforming.

The LGBTQ+ MENTORSHIP

For filmmakers who identify as LGBTQ+ (stands for lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, queer, and others).

The FILMMAKERS OF COLOUR MENTORSHIP

For filmmakers who identify as BIPOC (Black, Indigenous or People of Colour).

Growth and transformation

We partnered closely with the Watersprite team to help figuring out the tone that best fits their purpose, helping Watersprite to focus on what matters most: nurturing and empowering young talent in an inclusive space. Based on that premise, we developed an identity that truly express their mission, expressing the festival love and dedication to craft, transformation, nurturing and growth, in a kind and warm environment.

A warm and friendly direction inspired by cinema signage

We defined an art direction based on playful, friendly and warm tones, developing a grid system inspired on the cinema signs usually found on cinema entrances. The ellipse also plays a main role on Watersprite's identity, being the central element used to generate not only the water ripples but also a structural design element.
